Frequently Asked Questions

What is Pro Bono?

Pro Bono comes from the Latin expression "Pro bono publico", which means "for the public good" and is professional work undertaken without payment.

How can my organisation avail of your Pro Bono offer?

For Non-Profit organisations, my consulting services will always be free. However, in the case of For-Profit organisations, the circumstances will have to be evaluated.

It all starts with a free consultation that you can book here.

How does your starting fee model work?

How many times have you received a service/product and thought you paid too much for it?

That's why I set up this fee model. My bare minimum is €45 per hour. Then, as you receive my services and based on how you value them, you will determine the final price per hour.

What is bartering, and how does it work?

Barter is the act of trading goods or services without using money.

In this case, I provide you with my consulting services in return for your products/services in equal value to the number of worked hours at a price of €45 per hour.

What products/services do you accept when bartering?

There's no straight answer to this because life is constantly changing. As such, the option of payment by barter will be reviewed on a case by case basis.

When do you invoice for your services?

I send invoices per worked month. So if we didn't collaborate one month, then there's no invoice that month.

If you are paying for my services (not bartering), then before issuing the invoice, I will ask for your valuation of my services starting at €45 per hour.
